Stock Control Guide 2025

Classification: Internal Use Only

Section 1: Purpose & Scope

This SOP ensures accurate inventory counts, consistent ordering cycles, and minimal waste for all core product categories. It applies to receiving, storage, prep stations, and end-of-day reconciliation.

Critical KPI:
Target weekly waste below 2.5% of food cost and inventory variance below 1.2%.

Section 2: Restock Triggers and Cycle Counts

2.1 Minimum Par Levels

Category Par Level Reorder Trigger Emergency Threshold
Beef Patties 900 units 350 units 200 units
Chicken 500 units 180 units 100 units
Fries 700 units 250 units 150 units
Packaging 800 units 300 units 200 units

2.2 Cycle Count Schedule

  • Daily: Proteins, dairy, and produce.
  • Weekly: Dry goods and packaging.
  • Monthly: Equipment small parts and uniforms.
Do Not:
Never place new deliveries in front of older stock. Always follow FIFO rotation and label all items with the receiving date.

Section 3: Receiving and Storage

3.1 Delivery Check-In

  • Verify counts against invoice before accepting.
  • Log temperature on all refrigerated shipments.
  • Reject any damaged or out-of-temp product.

3.2 Storage Zones

  • Freezer: Raw proteins and frozen goods.
  • Cooler: Produce, dairy, and sauces.
  • Dry Storage: Packaging, napkins, and bulk items.

Section 4: Waste Control

Track all discarded items in the Waste Log with reason codes (expired, overcooked, contamination, or damage). Review waste totals at the end of each day.

Section 5: Reporting

Submit the Weekly Inventory Report by Sunday 6:00 PM with variance explanations and corrective actions.

Section 6: Emergency Reorder Playbook

  • When any critical item hits the emergency threshold, call the supplier within 30 minutes.
  • Temporarily reduce menu availability for low-stock items to protect core products.
  • Notify the shift lead and log the decision in the Inventory Notes Log.

Section 7: Cold Chain Monitoring
