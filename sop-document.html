Shift Management SOP

Holiday Rush Protocols 2025

Document ID: SOP-HR-2025-001
Version: 3.2 | Effective Date: December 1, 2025
Classification: Internal Use Only

Section 1: Purpose & Scope

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) provides comprehensive guidelines for managing McDonald's restaurant operations during high-traffic holiday periods, including Christmas Eve, New Year's Eve, and major sporting events (Super Bowl, NBA Finals, etc.).

Critical Success Factor:
Maintaining Customer Satisfaction above 80% during peak periods is mandatory to preserve brand reputation and ensure repeat business.

Section 2: Inventory Management

2.1 Pre-Event Stock Levels

When local events are expected to exceed 10,000 attendees, increase inventory by the following percentages:

Item Category Standard Stock Holiday Increase Critical Threshold
Burger Patties 850 units +20% 200 units
Chicken Breasts 420 units +25% 100 units
French Fries 650 units +30% 150 units
Beverage Syrup 380 boxes +15% 80 boxes
Packaging Supplies 720 units +25% 200 units
Common Mistake:
Do NOT over-order perishables beyond 48-hour capacity. Waste percentage must remain below 18% to maintain profitability.

2.2 Real-Time Monitoring

During rush periods, check inventory levels every 2 hours. If any item falls below the critical threshold, immediately contact the supplier for emergency delivery.

Section 3: Staff Management

3.1 Morale Maintenance

Staff morale directly impacts service quality. During high-stress periods:

  • Provide 15-minute breaks every 3 hours
  • Offer complimentary meals during extended shifts
  • Recognize exceptional performance publicly
  • Maintain morale above 50% to prevent turnover
Pro Tip:
A 10-point increase in staff morale can improve efficiency by up to 5% and customer satisfaction by 3-5 points.

3.2 Shift Scheduling

For events with expected attendance over 15,000:

  • Schedule minimum 2 additional staff per shift
  • Ensure Head Chef and Shift Supervisor overlap during peak hours (6 PM - 9 PM)
  • Have backup staff on-call for emergencies

Section 4: High Traffic Event Scenarios

4.1 NBA Finals / Super Bowl

Expected Impact: Customer flow increases by 30-40% during game hours and immediately post-game.

Recommended Actions:

  • Pre-cook 20% of standard burger patties 30 minutes before game end
  • Double beverage station capacity
  • Assign dedicated staff to queue management

4.2 Christmas Eve

Expected Impact: Family groups (4-6 people) increase by 50%. Higher quality expectations.

Recommended Actions:

  • Focus on presentation quality over speed
  • Increase packaging supplies by 25%
  • Offer seasonal promotions to boost reputation

4.3 Local Carnival / Concert

Expected Impact: Sudden spikes of 50-100 customers within 30-minute windows.

Recommended Actions:

  • Prioritize speed over customization
  • Pre-prepare popular combo meals
  • Ensure equipment is in optimal condition (80%+ efficiency)

Section 5: Equipment & Operations

5.1 Equipment Maintenance

Equipment condition below 50% can reduce efficiency by up to 30%. Schedule preventive maintenance:

  • Weekly: Clean fryers and grills thoroughly
  • Monthly: Inspect refrigeration units
  • Before Major Events: Full equipment check by certified technician

5.2 Efficiency Targets

Metric Target Range Critical Threshold
Customer Satisfaction 75-90% Below 60%
Staff Morale 60-85% Below 40%
Operational Efficiency 65-85% Below 50%
Waste Percentage 8-15% Above 20%

Section 6: Decision-Making Framework

When facing operational challenges, prioritize in this order:
  1. Customer Safety - Always first priority
  2. Customer Satisfaction - Protect brand reputation
  3. Staff Morale - Retain experienced team members
  4. Operational Efficiency - Maximize throughput
  5. Cost Management - Minimize waste and optimize budget

Section 7: Emergency Protocols

7.1 Supply Shortage

If critical inventory falls below threshold during peak hours:

  • Contact supplier immediately for emergency delivery
  • Temporarily remove affected items from menu
  • Offer substitutions with 10% discount to maintain satisfaction

7.2 Equipment Failure

If major equipment fails during rush:

  • Redirect orders to functional equipment
  • Reduce menu to items that can be prepared with available equipment
  • Call maintenance technician immediately
  • Consider temporary closure if customer satisfaction drops below 40%
