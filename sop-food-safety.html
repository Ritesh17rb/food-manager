Food Safety & Sanitation SOP

Daily Cleanliness Standards 2025

Document ID: SOP-FS-2025-004
Version: 2.4 | Effective Date: January 15, 2025
Classification: Internal Use Only

Section 1: Purpose & Scope

This SOP defines the daily food safety, sanitation, and hygiene standards required for all McDonald's locations. It applies to kitchen production, storage, service counters, restrooms, and dining areas.

Critical Standard:
All food-contact surfaces must be sanitized every 2 hours or immediately after contamination. Target inspection score: 95% or higher.

Section 2: Hygiene and Temperature Controls

2.1 Handwashing Requirements

  • Wash hands for at least 20 seconds with warm water and soap.
  • After handling raw product, trash, cleaning chemicals, or personal items.
  • Use disposable gloves for ready-to-eat items and change every 30 minutes.

2.2 Temperature Standards

Item Safe Temperature Check Frequency
Cooked Beef 155F minimum Every batch
Cooked Chicken 165F minimum Every batch
Holding Cabinets 140F minimum Hourly
Cold Storage 41F maximum Start/end of shift
Critical Warning:
Any item found in the danger zone (41F to 140F) for over 30 minutes must be discarded and logged.

2.3 Sanitizer Concentration

Test sanitizer buckets with test strips at the start of each shift and every 4 hours:

  • Quaternary sanitizer: 150-400 ppm
  • Chlorine sanitizer: 50-100 ppm

Section 3: Daily Cleaning Checklist

3.1 Opening Tasks

  • Sanitize prep counters, grills, and fryer stations.
  • Calibrate temperature probes and log results.
  • Inspect sinks, soap dispensers, and paper towel stations.

3.2 Mid-Shift Tasks

  • Wipe high-touch surfaces (door handles, POS screens, condiment stations).
  • Clean and sanitize beverage nozzles every 4 hours.
  • Empty and sanitize trash receptacles before they reach 75% capacity.

3.3 Closing Tasks

  • Disassemble and clean grill plates and fryer baskets.
  • Wipe refrigeration seals and storage racks.
  • Dispose of expired or unlabeled items.

Section 4: Cross-Contamination Prevention

  • Use color-coded cutting boards and tools for raw vs. ready-to-eat items.
  • Store raw proteins on the bottom shelves of coolers.
  • Never use the same utensil for allergens without sanitizing.

Section 5: Documentation

Complete the Daily Sanitation Log before shift end. Any missed tasks require a manager sign-off and a corrective action note.

Section 6: Audit & Verification

Audit Type Frequency Owner Pass Threshold
Manager Walkthrough Daily (open + close) Shift Manager 95%
Food Safety Checklist Weekly Assistant Manager 97%
Internal Compliance Review Monthly GM 98%

Section 7: Incident Response

  • Isolate any suspect food item and label "Do Not Use".
  • Dispose of items held in the danger zone over 30 minutes.
  • Complete an Incident Report within 1 hour of discovery.
  • Notify the manager on duty and document corrective actions.
