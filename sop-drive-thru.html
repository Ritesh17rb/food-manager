Drive-Thru Operations SOP

Speed of Service Playbook 2025

Document ID: SOP-DT-2025-003
Version: 4.2 | Effective Date: March 1, 2025
Classification: Internal Use Only

Section 1: Purpose & Scope

This SOP standardizes drive-thru service flow to achieve fast, accurate, and friendly experiences. It covers order taking, payment, staging, handoff, and throughput management during peak periods.

Speed Target:
Average drive-thru total time must remain below 170 seconds during peak hours with order accuracy above 97%.

Section 2: Order Taking Standards

2.1 Greeting Script

Use the 10-second greeting rule and confirm each combo:

  • "Welcome to McDonald's. May I take your order?"
  • Repeat modifiers and sizes back to the guest.
  • Confirm beverages and sauces before closing the order.

2.2 Accuracy Checklist

Step Action Owner
Order Build Match screen to bag label Assembler
Verification 2-point check (main + side) Expeditor
Handoff Confirm sauces and drinks Window 2
Common Error:
Never skip the verbal confirmation. Mismatched sauces are the top driver of drive-thru complaints.

Section 3: Peak Hour Throughput

3.1 Staging Rules

  • Stage large or custom orders in the pull-forward bay.
  • Keep Window 2 free for the next two cars when possible.
  • Use a runner when the queue exceeds 6 cars.

3.2 Lane Assignments

  • Window 1: Payments and order confirmation.
  • Window 2: Handoff and final accuracy check.
  • Runner: Pull-forward orders and curbside delivery during surges.

Section 4: Metrics and Coaching

Managers review the Speed of Service dashboard every 30 minutes and coach when total time exceeds 180 seconds for two consecutive checks.

4.1 Performance Thresholds

Metric Target Action at Risk
Total Time 170s Staff a runner + pre-stage items
Order Accuracy 97% Assign expeditor to verify bags
Window 2 Dwell 45s Shift to pull-forward for large orders
