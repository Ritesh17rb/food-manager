Section 1: De-escalation and Recovery Steps

This SOP outlines how to handle guest complaints quickly, respectfully, and consistently to preserve brand trust. It applies to in-store, drive-thru, delivery, and digital order issues.

3-Minute Rule:
Every complaint must be acknowledged within 3 minutes of being reported.

Section 2: The L.A.S.T. Method

2.1 L.A.S.T. Steps

  • Listen without interruption and confirm understanding.
  • Apologize and accept responsibility on behalf of the team.
  • Solve with a clear resolution option.
  • Thank the guest for feedback and invite them back.

2.2 Recovery Options

Issue Type Standard Resolution Approval Needed
Incorrect order Replace item + small fries No
Excessive wait Coupon for next visit Shift Lead
Quality complaint Replace meal + drink Manager
Do Not:
Never argue with a guest. Escalate to a manager if resolution exceeds $10 value or if a safety concern is raised.

Section 3: Documentation

Log all complaints in the Guest Recovery Log with the date, issue type, resolution, and team member involved. Capture any repeat issues for escalation.

Section 4: Follow-up

Review complaint trends in the weekly manager meeting and adjust training as needed. Repeat complaints require a corrective action plan within 5 business days.

Section 5: Digital and Delivery Complaints

  • Verify order ID and delivery partner before resolving.
  • Provide a digital coupon within 24 hours when required.
  • Report missing or tampered seals to the delivery vendor immediately.

Section 6: Escalation Matrix

Scenario Escalate To Response Time
Safety concern or allergy risk Store Manager Immediate
Refund request over $10 Shift Manager Within 5 minutes
Repeat complaint within 7 days General Manager Within 24 hours

Section 7: Training & Quality Assurance

  • Review the top 3 complaint drivers every week with the team.
  • Role-play the L.A.S.T. method during pre-shift huddles.
  • Track recovery satisfaction score and target 90%+.
